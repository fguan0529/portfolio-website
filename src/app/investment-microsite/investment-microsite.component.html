Introduction

Initially, the sponsor did not know the exact product they wanted. They did know that they wanted to connect with new target audience that the company did not reach due to their communication style of their existing website and material and lack of marketing. In the initial stages of the project, we had a little miscommunication with the sponsor in terms of expectations of the project. The sponsor initially expected more marketing content out of us while we, the team, were expecting to deliver a more technical approach that our skillsets would provide. We were able to overcome this and communicate more concisely with our sponsor moving forward.

Mission Statement

The microsite should fulfill our three main goals: to clearly explain the company's core values and investing style to a new audience, to express the unique advantages of company's investing style, and to deliver the key points of information in an easy and digestible manner.

Design Process

Even though we worked in Agile, the sponsor wanted separate design and implementation phases to first deliver design deliverables before starting implementation. So, for the first few sprints, we delivered research, sketches, mockups and, finally, a prototype to work off of. Much of the planning revolved around the marketing aspect as well, figuring out how to thoroughly inform as well as engage the user with the company's investing services as they interact with the microsite.


Personas

The sponsor explained to us that the audience they wanted the product to connect to were young professionals as well as older women nearing retirement. They felt they currently lack any connection to these target groups. Initial plans also considered the three groups of user types that the company works with: investor, advisor and institution. These user types were taken into consideration in our personas. The project itself ended up only focusing on investors in later iterations.

The Problem

The company's current website information is hard to understand for non-industry individuals and those new to the investing. The lack of marketing also contributes to the resulting gap in their client demographic, leaving an untapped source of potential new investors.

Website Flow Plan

Instead of overwhelming a user with too much information and background when they arrive at the website, we wanted to provide interactive features that allow them to familiarize themselves with the company and provide them resources. The user journey should start with interest, then take minimal input from the user, and output personalized information and resources. This way, the user gains a good amount of information with only a simple amount of engagement with the website.

1. Visual

The visual serves as the attention grabber at the top of the website. It should be something the user can relate to as well as capture their attention to sway them to continue browsing the website.

2. Introduction

The introduction includes a quick overview of all the features of the website as and gives the user a reason to continue interacting with the website. This is also where a background on the company and its investing style should be introduced to the user in an easily digestible manner.

3. Funds Screener

With the Funds Screener, the user is given the chance to answer a few questions about themselves and their financial goals. The screener would then provide them with a few of the company's mutual funds to start and simple explanations on why they are a good fit for the user based on their answers.

4. Portfolio Optimizer

The Portfolio Optimizer takes it to the next step where the user has the option to input the funds they were just recommended from the Funds Screener, or any of their own choosing. The optimizer will then suggest a third stock that would help them diversify and balance their portfolio.

5. Advisor Locator

Lastly, equipped with their sample portfolio, the user can locate a financial advisor to help them get started with investing with the company's funds and services.

Note

A better solution the company could implement as the final call to action would be to allow users to directly connect to a financial advisor online. However, the company is only responsible for managing the funds and is not a brokerage, so there is a lack infrastructure to implement such a feature.

Our Solution

The microsite provides an informative introduction to the company and their investment services. The engaging tools on the website leaves the user well-equipped with the knowledge to give them the confidence take the call to action: to meet a financial advisor about investing with the company's funds.
