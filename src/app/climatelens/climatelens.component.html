Introduction

For this project, we were tasked with picking an upcoming technology and to create a product with it that addressed a current social issue. Our team chose the technology of augmented reality to address the social issue of climate change.

Mission Statement

Our solution will use AR simulations of climate change disasters to spur motivation in individuals to actively participate in the fight against climate change and provide guidance that is specifically tailored to the their location and lifestyle.

Logo

To reflect our app's goal of providing a closer look at the world around us through AR, the logo was created with the idea of looking at our world through a lens.

Lean Canvas

The lean canvas acted as our scaffolding of the project, fleshing out the idea and goals of the product.

The Problem

The problem is not lack of awareness anymore, but lack of urgency about climate change.

Value Proposition

We defined the value our product would bring to our target audience. This helped shape the features we would like out product to provide in relation to what our users would want.

Our Solution

To give users the tools, resources, and motivation to reduce their individual negative impact on the environment with a central information hub and community for activists.

User Journey

The journey we envisioned for users was one where they started curious and ended with motivation to take action. Our app would provide the resources to make acting on their motivation as easy and accessible as possible.

The "Map" section would facilitate the AR interactive educational feature. The "Participate" section would show news and volunteer opportunities from local organizations. The "My Pledges" section would provide tips and ideas to improve personal environmental conscious habits and a bullet journal style tracking system.

Design


Prototype

The initial digital prototypes were created in Adobe Photoshop utilizing its mobile canvas sizes and art board features. The prototype featured the three main views of the application: "Participate," "Map," and "My Pledges." How the basic features for the application would look were included in the prototype.


Splash Screen

The theme of the splash screen and logo was an icy palette to imitate the ice caps that are one of the most recognizable entities that are directly affected by climate change that users would find familiar.


Onboarding

To introduce the application to users, we included some simple onboarding screens that briefly explain the applications functionality. In this iteration, we tailored the text to a user that would have been introduced to the app through social media or another avenue and may already have some prior knowledge to what the app does. Future iterations should also take into consideration users with no prior knowledge.


Map & AR

Inspired by the most popular AR app on the market, Pokemon Go, we wanted users to be able to explore and learn about the environment around them with fact blurbs that pop up and allowing them to simulate how the environment would change with different water and temperature levels as well as through time. This was the rather ambitious feature of the product concept as it would need an extensive amount of environmental data to simulate the AR. Therefore, we also had different AR ideas that could be explored in future iterations of the design such as making it more game-like with item collecting or tree planting while keeping the factual blurbs.


Participate

The idea behind the "Participate" section was for it to be the central hub of information and resources. We wanted article and organization suggestions under "Explore" and have separate sections specifically for organizations, pledges, articles and a log of all pages the user "liked." A different way to do it in future iterations would be to have the four sections that have giant buttons to turn into tabbed views in the "Participate" section for easier navigation between the pages.


My Pledges

The "My Pledges" section emulates a bullet journal style habit tracker for users to keep track of any new habits they wanted to adapt to be more environmentally conscious. We also included a "My Badges" feature in the prototype for users to earn achievements as they use the habit tracker.
